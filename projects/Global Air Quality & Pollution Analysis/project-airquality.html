Key Findings

What the data revealed

🌧

South Asia Epicenter

Lahore and Dhaka lead global PM2.5 rankings. The Indo-Gangetic Plain shows a systemic regional issue driven by agricultural burning, dense traffic, and industrial activity — not isolated local problems.

206.99 µg/m³
❄

Winter Peak Pattern

A clear seasonal cycle emerges every year — PM2.5 and PM10 spike dramatically in November–February. January 2025 recorded the highest PM2.5 levels at 63.3 µg/m³, driven by cold air trapping pollutants near the surface.

Nov → Feb Peak
💧

Combustion Triad

NO2 and PM2.5 show an extremely strong correlation of 0.87, and CO with SO2 at 0.80. These pollutants share a common combustion source profile — vehicles and industry are the primary drivers.

r = 0.87
⚡

2026 Pollution Surge

PM2.5 rose from 49.33 in 2025 to 56.08 in 2026 — a 13.7% jump in a single year. This suggests industrial expansion or relaxed environmental regulations have outpaced green initiatives globally.

+13.7% in 1yr
📈

High Intra-Day Volatility

Many cities show daily max-minus-min gaps exceeding 50–100 µg/m³. A city with a "Moderate" daily average can contain 4-hour windows of "Hazardous" exposure — daily averages mask dangerous peaks.

50–100 µg/m³ gap
🅾

ML Model Performance

XGBoost achieves R²=0.878 predicting median PM2.5 from temperature, humidity, pressure, NO2, O3 and other pollutants — without using any PM2.5 data as input features (no data leakage).

R² = 0.878
